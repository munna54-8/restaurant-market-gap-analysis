Kochi Restaurant Market: Cuisine Demand-Gap Analysis

Which cuisines are underserved relative to demand among Kochi's most prominent restaurants — and which are the most crowded? Analysis of 90 listings scraped across 10 Kochi localities on Zomato, deduplicated to 33 unique restaurants.

A note on scope 63% of the initially scraped listings (57 of 90) turned out to be duplicates — the same restaurants appearing under multiple "nearby" localities, since Zomato's per-area listings draw from a shared citywide pool rather than fully distinct rankings. After deduplication, this analysis covers Kochi's 33 most prominent, most-reviewed restaurants — a meaningful but not exhaustive slice of the city's full restaurant market.

Key findings

  • Gap Burger (+73.1) and Coffee (+61.5) show the strongest demand-to-supply imbalance — real whitespace for a new concept.
  • Saturation Kerala (-73.1) and South Indian (-57.7) are the most common cuisines in the dataset, but score lowest on demand-per-restaurant — the "safe" default is also the most crowded.
  • Data quality 63% of scraped listings were duplicates across localities — itself a finding about how Zomato's recommendation widget works, not just a cleaning step.
  • Popularity ≠ quality Review count and rating are essentially uncorrelated (-0.039) — a heavily-reviewed restaurant is no more likely to be highly rated than a lightly-reviewed one.

Recommendation

Among Kochi's most prominent restaurants, Burger and Coffee-led concepts show meaningfully more demand relative to how many currently exist — genuine whitespace for a new entrant. A new Kerala or South Indian restaurant, by contrast, would be entering the most crowded and most commoditized segment of the market, where standing out will be harder regardless of execution quality.
